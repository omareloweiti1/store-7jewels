South America

The land of gold jewelry and precious stones. South America continues to be the heart of gold production for jewelry and is home to some of the most stunning precious gemstones, from green serpentines to opals and emeralds.

Australia

Jewelry played a crucial role in establishing the Australian identity, taking inspiration from the continent’s dynamic climate and landscape. The whimsical flora and fauna found in the Australian bushland are common motifs in jewelry. Indigenous Australian culture continues to influence jewelry today, from shell bracelets to woven necklaces.

Europe

Every corner of Europe has influenced modern jewelry design. Responsible for the romanticization of jewelry, Europe is the birthplace of some of the most famous jewelry designs in the world, from lockets to cameos and baroque pearls. Many of today’s jewelry trends can be traced back to Medieval Europe.

Asia

While Asia is famous for its iconic Silk Road, the continent produces some of the most unique and versatile jewelry that pays homage to its culture. Jade bracelets take their beauty from the natural imperfections of the stone. Pearl jewelry is synonymous with Asia, where the semi-precious stone was dived for by hand.

North America

Home of Hollywood and the most iconic jewelry brands. Pioneers of American jewelry continue to influence trends today, from the iconic little blue box to diamonds that dazzle on the silver screen. North American jewelry takes a more minimalistic approach, driving the Art Deco period and creating jewelry that serves as modern heirlooms.

Africa

The first continent where jewelry wearing was popularized. African jewelry is famous for its vibrant colors, textural materials, and rich craftsmanship. Each piece tells a story of African culture, infused with symbolism. Although Africa’s history with jewelry dates back thousands of years, its famous bead culture has stood the test of time. Africa’s ancient heritage continues to influence jewelry designs today, taking inspiration from countries like Ethiopia and Egypt.
